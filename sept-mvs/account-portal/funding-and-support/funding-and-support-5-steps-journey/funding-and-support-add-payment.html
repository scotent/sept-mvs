BACS

See examples

Add a Bacs or payroll statement that shows the salary payments for each of the employees you are claiming for. If you pay your employees directly from your bank account, you don't need to include any files here.

If the costs you are claiming appear as part of a single bulk Bacs payment on your bank statement (for example, as 'Payroll'), then you must submit a Bacs transaction list, as well as a bank statement as evidence of payment. Any transactions not relevant to your claim can be redacted (obscured or scored through).

The Bacs transaction list must:

  • Be clearly identifiable as coming from your payment or payroll system
  • Clearly show individual payees, payment amounts and dates
Please note: The total payment sum and payment date on the transaction list must match the amount and date that appears on your bank statement. Any transactions not relevant to your claim can be redacted (obscured or scored through).

Bank statement

See examples

Add a bank statement that shows the total of the Bacs statement you have added, or shows the salary payments of each employee you are claiming for.

Any transactions not relevant to your claim can be redacted (obscured or scored through).

For a bank statement to be fully compliant as evidence of payment, it must:

  • Be a formal statement (either paper copy or online printout), not simply a transaction listing
  • Clearly show the bank name, address and logo
  • Clearly show the account holder's name and details
  • Clearly show relevant transaction dates, amounts and payees that correspond to other evidence, such as invoices, salary payments or receipts.
